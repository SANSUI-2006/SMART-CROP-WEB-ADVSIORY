Design a production-ready web application UI/UX for an agriculture intelligence platform focused on farmers, field officers, agri-cooperatives, and local market stakeholders in Nashik and Ratnagiri, Maharashtra, with offline-first functionality and full multilingual support in English and Marathi. The application must feel realistic, implementable, and complete — not conceptual only. The product should help users manage crop data, monitor regional crop conditions, compare market trends, access pest and disease guidance, review weather-linked alerts, and work even in low-network rural conditions. The interface should look polished enough for a final-year project, startup pitch, and real deployment demo.

The product vision is: “From field data to action” — a practical tool that transforms scattered agricultural information into usable decisions for farmers and local administrators. The UI should communicate reliability, field readiness, and modern intelligence, while keeping usability simple for non-technical rural users. The design must feel hands-on, action-oriented, and structured, similar to the presentation’s emphasis on practical work, modular tracks, collaboration, learning, and real-world execution.

Product concept
Create a web app named something like AgriSetu, CropPulse Maharashtra, or KrishiMitra Smart, with the final naming style left premium, modern, and regionally relevant. The app must support importing and managing datasets from Nashik and Ratnagiri for crops such as mango, cashew, onion, tomato, grapes, pomegranate, rice, coconut, jackfruit, banana, chilli, and other locally relevant crops, with crop-region mapping and expandable datasets. The system should clearly distinguish regional strengths, such as Ratnagiri for mango and cashew, and Nashik for onion, tomato, grapes, and pomegranate, through elegant visual storytelling and data cards.

The application should work in offline mode, allowing users to continue viewing cached crop data, saved dashboards, recent alerts, local language content, and previously synced advisory modules without internet. Include a sync status system, “last updated” indicators, conflict handling for edited field entries, and a graceful offline banner. Make it clear in the design that this is a major competitive advantage over many agriculture platforms that depend heavily on constant connectivity.

Core features
Include these major modules in the app design:

Multilingual UI toggle with instant switch between English and Marathi, preserving layout quality in both languages.

Offline-first dashboard with cached widgets, sync indicators, and downloadable regional datasets.

Dataset import center for CSV, Excel, government agriculture data sheets, mandi price files, and local survey uploads.

Regional crop intelligence by district, taluka, village, crop type, season, soil type, and irrigation condition.

Crop profile pages for mango, cashew, onion, tomato, grapes, and more, including sowing/planting cycles, disease risks, ideal conditions, yield range, and market trend snapshots.

Smart comparison tools to compare crops by profitability, water need, disease risk, region suitability, and market demand.

Pest and disease advisory with visual cards, prevention steps, treatment options, and urgency labels.

Weather-aware recommendations linked to crop stage and district conditions.

Farmer record management for land parcel, crop history, input use, and advisory notes.

Market insights section with mandi trends, crop price movement, seasonal demand, and district-level sales opportunities.

Alerts and notifications for pest outbreaks, rainfall warnings, disease spread, and mandi price change.

Admin panel for dataset upload, language content editing, crop library management, and user role administration.

Field officer mode for collecting data in villages and syncing when internet returns.
